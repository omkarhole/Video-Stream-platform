# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We pledge that our community will be a harassment-free experience for everyone, and we are dedicated to providing a positive, constructive, and safe environment for all participants.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Supporting newcomers in learning and growing
- Taking responsibility for mistakes and offering sincere apologies
- Helping others succeed and learn

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental health, neuroatypicality, physical appearance, body size, race, age, regional discrimination, or religion
- Unwelcome comments regarding a person's lifestyle choices and practices
- Deliberate and intentional disrespect
- Sustained disruption of community spaces
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Physical contact without consent
- Threats of violence, violent language, or violent jokes
- Sharing of non-consensual intimate images
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks or public shaming
- Spamming or promotional content without permission
- Deliberately publishing others' private information without consent ("doxing")
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All community spaces including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and code comments
- Official community events and gatherings
- Any representation of the project in public (social media, conferences, etc.)
- Private communications that relate to project business

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or the community.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have other concerns, please report it by emailing the project maintainers at **[contact email]**. 

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information (so we can follow up with you)
- Names/usernames of the people involved
- Description of the incident
- Where it occurred
- When it occurred
- Any additional context or evidence

All reports will be:
- Reviewed promptly and thoroughly
- Handled with discretion and sensitivity
- Investigated fairly with input from all parties involved
- Kept confidential where possible

### No Retaliation

We prohibit retaliation against anyone for making a good faith report of conduct violations, or for cooperating with investigations.

### Consequences

Community members who violate this Code of Conduct may face:

1. **Warning:** A private message explaining the violation and required changes
2. **Temporary Ban:** Removal from community spaces for a specified period
3. **Permanent Ban:** Indefinite removal from community spaces

The severity of consequences will be determined based on:
- Nature and severity of the violation
- Pattern of behavior
- Intent of the violator
- Impact on the affected person(s) and community
- Response to feedback

## Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly treated or falsely accused:

1. Contact the project maintainers with your appeal
2. Provide your perspective and any evidence
3. Receive a fair review of your case
4. Be notified of the outcome

## Guidance for Maintainers

### Addressing Violations

When addressing Code of Conduct violations:

- **Listen actively** to all parties involved
- **Investigate thoroughly** before taking action
- **Respond promptly** to reports
- **Be transparent** about consequences
- **Document** all incidents and actions
- **Follow due process** fairly and consistently

### Creating Inclusive Spaces

Maintainers should:

- Welcome newcomers warmly
- Lead by example in respectful communication
- Address violations promptly
- Foster psychological safety
- Celebrate diversity
- Provide support resources

## Community Resources

### Getting Help

If you need support or resources:

- **Mental Health:** Consult a mental health professional if harassment has affected your wellbeing
- **Safety Concerns:** Contact local authorities if you face threats or physical danger
- **Community Support:** Reach out to other community members you trust

### Learning and Growth

- Educate yourself about inclusive practices
- Listen to feedback and perspectives from marginalized communities
- Attend workshops on diversity and inclusion
- Reflect on your own biases and behaviors
